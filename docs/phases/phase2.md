## Commodity Exclusion Rationale

### Why Uranium Was Excluded

Despite bullish narrative (supply deficits, nuclear renaissance, Goldman Sachs $91/lb forecast):

1. **Extreme volatility** in thin spot market driven by speculation
2. **Execution risk** in nuclear project timelines (regulatory, public opposition)
3. **Geopolitical concentration** with Russian supply (26% global)
4. **Long investment horizon** required (multi-year to decade)
5. **Limited accessibility** for retail investors
6. **Regulatory uncertainty** around nuclear policy

**Conclusion:** May warrant small thematic allocation (2-5%) for aggressive investors, but not core recommendation.

### Why Palladium Was Excluded

Despite 83% surge in 2025 and bullish near-term outlook:

1. **Structural demand decline** from EV adoption (irreversible long-term trend)
2. **Substitution risk** with platinum (WPIC forecasts 250 koz reverse substitution by 2029)
3. **Policy dependence** on continued EV tax credit reversals
4. **Supply moving to surplus** after 2026 per WPIC forecasts
5. **Geopolitical risk** from U.S. ITC investigation of Russian imports (40% of U.S. imports)
6. **Narrow use case** concentrated in automotive catalytic converters (80%+ demand)
7. **Timing risk** after sharp 2025 rally; Metals Focus forecasts $1,150 by Q4 2026

**Conclusion:** May offer tactical trading opportunities, but structural headwinds preclude strategic recommendation.
